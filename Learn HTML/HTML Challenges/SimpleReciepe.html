Chicken Tikka Masala

A quintessential North Indian dish, Chicken Tikka Masala is beloved for its creamy, spiced tomato-based sauce and tender, flavorful chicken.

Ingredients
  • 1 lb (about 450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
Steps
  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with all the marinade ingredients. Mix well to ensure the chicken is evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight for best results.
  2. Cook the chicken: Heat a large skillet or pan over medium-high heat. You can either grill the chicken pieces on skewers or pan-fry them until cooked through and slightly charred on the edges. Set the cooked chicken aside.
  3. Prepare the masala: In the same skillet, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
  4. Sauté the aromatics: Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  5. Add the spices: Stir in the garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, and turmeric powder. Cook for about 30 seconds until the spices are aromatic.
  6. Simmer the sauce: Pour in the tomato purée and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the oil starts to separate.
  7. Combine and finish: Add the cooked chicken to the sauce and stir to coat. Pour in the heavy cream or coconut milk and season with salt. Let it simmer for another 5 minutes.
  8. Garnish and serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with naan bread or basmati rice.

Dal Makhani

A rich and creamy lentil dish from the Punjab region, Dal Makhani is a staple at celebrations and a favorite in Indian restaurants worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, puréed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 4 tablespoons butter (makhani)
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Soak the lentils and beans:Wash the black lentils and kidney beans thoroughly and soak them in water overnight.
  2. Cook the lentils and beans:Drain the water and transfer the lentils and beans to a pressure cooker. Add fresh water, salt, and a pinch of turmeric powder. Pressure cook until they are very soft and tender (usually about 10-12 whistles).
  3. Prepare the tempering:In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat the butter over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Sauté the aromatics:Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Then, add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies and cook for another minute.
  5. Cook the tomatoes: Pour in the tomato purée and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  6. Add the spices: Stir in the red chili powder and garam masala.
  7. Combine and simmer: Add the cooked lentils and beans to the pan. Mash some of the lentils against the side of the pan to thicken the gravy. Add some water if the consistency is too thick. Let the dal simmer on low heat for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld.
  8. Finish and serve: Stir in the heavy cream and chopped cilantro. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
